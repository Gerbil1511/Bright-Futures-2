Our Mission

"At Bright Futures, our mission is to educate and inspire children to embrace diversity, inclusion, and social justice through creative and engaging programs, fostering a more compassionate, empathetic, and inclusive society for all."

Bright Futures ambassadors are the heart of our organization.

These dedicated individuals are trained to deliver interactive and inspiring sessions in schools, tailored specifically for elementary age children. Using stories, games, and activities, our ambassadors make learning about diversity and inclusion a fun and engaging experience.

Understanding Diversity & Inclusion

What is Diversity?

Diversity means understanding that everyone is different. Whether they come from different backgrounds, different countries, or more. Diversity encompasses everything from ethnicity, gender identity, sex, sexuality, race, religion, and economic status amongst others.

A diverse community is a wonderful thing that should be celebrated. It ensures that everyone is free to be themselves without being fearful of prejudice. It also allows us to build relationships with others while embracing what makes us all unique.

What is Inclusion?

Inclusion is when everyone feels welcome and included, no matter how different they are. Imagine you're playing a game with your friends, and you make sure that every single person gets to join in and have fun. You don't leave anyone out, and you make sure everyone feels happy and part of the group.

In the same way, inclusion means making sure that everyone, no matter what they look like, where they come from, or what they can do, feels like they belong. It's about being kind, respectful, and making sure everyone has an equal chance to participate and enjoy things together.

Ethnic Representation in Children's Literature

The Centre for Literacy in Primary Education's "Reflecting Realities" report shows 30% of children's titles now feature racially minoritised characters, up from 4% in 2017.

Nearly 2 in 5 children find it difficult to find books with characters like them, especially among Black ethnic backgrounds and those receiving free school meals.

Activities

View All

Invisible Clay

What is it?: Non-verbal communication game where players 'mould' invisible clay while others guess what they're creating.

Learning Objective: Introduces non-verbal communication through gestures and facial expressions, helping children understand that communication goes beyond words.

Duration: 15-20 minutes | Ages: 5+

Reverse Musical Chairs

A twist on the classic game where the group must find ways to include everyone when the music stops.

Learning Objective: Teaches creative problem-solving for inclusion as the challenge increases with each round.

Duration: 10-15 minutes | Ages: 4+

Culture Share Circle

Children bring an item representing their culture and take turns sharing its significance.

Learning Objective: Promotes cultural awareness and appreciation of diverse backgrounds in a personal, engaging way.

Duration: 20-30 minutes | Ages: 5+

Kindness Chain

Students write acts of kindness they've done or received on paper strips to build a classroom chain.

Learning Objective: Visually demonstrates how small acts of kindness connect us all, regardless of differences.

Duration: Ongoing | Ages: 4+

Similarities Web

Using a ball of yarn, students create a web by sharing similarities while tossing the yarn across the circle.

Learning Objective: Creates a visual representation of how we're connected through shared experiences and qualities.

Duration: 15-20 minutes | Ages: 5+
